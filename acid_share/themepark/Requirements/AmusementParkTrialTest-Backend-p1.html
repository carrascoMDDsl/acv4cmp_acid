Welcome to the CM Productions AMUSEMENT PARK trial test (page 1/2)

This page is about what we provide to you in this trial task.

Introduction

  • Imagine you are working for an amusement park with attractions and rides.
  • Imagine that the park is installing a huge screen near each attraction, or imagine that the park is doing a free mobile application.
  • In any case, the park wants to let the users know how long they should wait before riding an attraction.

What you get

  • You will get html+css+javascript code that simulates what the bosses expect.
  • You will have to model in PHP the business objects that play a role in the backend.
  • In the sample html you're gonna see:
    1. Attractions of several colors over a map.
    2. The roads connecting the attractions.
    3. The crossings between roads.
  • This is merely a sample code. In an MVC approach, this could be rendered by a templating system that the controller invokes passing to it the data that gets from the model. You will be focused in the modelling part.

Try the sample code

  • Get it now - Download it here.
  • Uncompress.
  • Open the index.html page from a browser.
  • You will see a map of the park with attractions.
  • There are 2 information sources:
    • The static information (like attractions or roads positions) is stored in a database. - See the structure in the "sql" directory
    • The real-time information (like if the attractions have a large queue or are free) comes from a JSON API.
  • You also have a "SeaWorld Attraction Diagram.jpg" inside a folder, with the IDs of the attractions, roads, and crossings.
At the time of the interview you will get more information.
